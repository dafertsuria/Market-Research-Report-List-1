<p><p>Plasma Chemical Vapor Deposition (PCVD) is a technique used to deposit thin films on substrates through plasma-enhanced chemical reactions. The market is segmented into RF Plasma and DC Plasma types. RF Plasma utilizes radio frequency energy to generate high-frequency plasma, allowing for uniform coating over complex shapes, while DC Plasma relies on direct current to create plasma, generally favored for simpler applications. Both methods are crucial for industries such as electronics, optics, and solar cells, enhancing material properties and performance.</p></p>
