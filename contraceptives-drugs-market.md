<p><p>The contraceptive drugs market is categorized into three main types: long-acting, short-acting, and emergency contraceptives. Long-acting contraceptives include methods like IUDs and implants that provide protection for extended periods, often years. Short-acting contraceptives, such as pills, patches, and injections, require more frequent administration and offer flexibility. Emergency contraceptives are used post-intercourse to prevent pregnancy and are vital for unexpected situations. Each category serves distinct needs, contributing to diverse contraceptive choices for individuals.</p></p>
